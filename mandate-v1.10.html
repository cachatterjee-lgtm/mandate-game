The premise

Month 1. A contractor slides an envelope across your desk. ₹3 lakh. Monthly. He has held the sanitation contract for 8 years. Refuse, and the garbage trucks stop running. Accept, and you are already inside a system that never stays at a fixed price.

You have a ward to run. 45,000 residents. Broken drains. Potholed roads. A party that helped you win and now expects something in return. And ₹5 lakh of discretionary funds to do anything about any of it.

Some months bring crises — a flood, a tanker mafia, a WhatsApp rumour that could turn violent. Some bring choices — build the road before elections, or the clinic that nobody photographs. Between crises, you govern: spend, apply for central schemes, decide what the ward actually needs versus what it will remember you for.

Every scenario is fictional. Every law cited is real. Every consequence follows from choices you actually made. There is no clean path. There is only the ward, the system, and who you decide to be inside it.

How it works
01
Pick your background
The Reformer (public trust, no party muscle), the Inheritor (family connections, public skepticism), or the Builder (self-funded, balanced start). Each begins with a different hand. The game is always the same.
02
Respond to events
Major decisions arrive across all 5 years — a contractor's bribe in Month 1, a tanker mafia in Year 3, a ₹2 crore offer 48 hours before election. Each choice is tagged: Reform, Corrupt, or Compromise. Each has consequences that arrive months later.
03
Govern between crises
Open the Governance Dashboard anytime to spend on projects — roads, streetlights, health camps, a sports ground. You start with ₹5L per quarter. Central schemes like AMRUT and Khelo India unlock bigger projects if you apply early enough. Voters notice what you build — and what you don't.
04
Watch inaction cost you
Every quiet month without investment, approval drifts down. First slowly, then faster. Two warnings arrive before the drop becomes serious. In Year 4-5, inaction is twice as expensive. The ward does not wait forever.
05
Unlock real laws
After every major decision, the law that governs the situation unlocks — RTI Act 2005, Street Vendors Act 2014, 74th Constitutional Amendment, Article 21. You understand a law better when you have already struggled with the problem it tried to solve.
06
Earn your tier
Month 60. Five years calculated. Your archetype is determined by how you governed — not just how you scored. Platinum is rare and hard to reach. The Fallen is always avoidable. Share your result and see if anyone can do better.

How the score is calculated
Public Approval
62%
How much your ward residents trust and support you. Weighted average across all 7 voter groups. Max: 100.
Party Support
50%
How much your political party values your loyalty and effectiveness. Completely separate from voters. Max: 100.
The party does not care if voters love you. Voters do not care if the party loves you. You need both. This is the central tension of democratic politics. Your combined score adds the two together out of 200.

Governance Dashboard
Active Focus Area
Your focus area shapes how the ward responds to your governance style. Select one — it applies a passive bonus to matching projects and changes how story events affect you.

Infrastructure First — Roads and drainage cost 15% less. Flood/pothole events hurt you less.
Social Welfare — Health camps cost ₹0.6L instead of ₹0.8L. Slum and women approval decays 50% slower.
Economic Growth — Traders get passive +3 approval. Market projects complete faster.
Transparency & Reform — Corrupt choices cost 20% more party capital. RTI events generate positive press.
Party Loyalty — Party capital costs 25% less. MLA demands are smaller. Shield triggers at 55% instead of 60%.
Balanced — No bonus, no penalty. Maximum flexibility.
